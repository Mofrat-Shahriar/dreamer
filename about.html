Biography

Hello, I'm
Mofrat Shahriar

I'm a self-taught photographer from Bangladesh with a deep passion for finding quiet beauty in the everyday world. My photography journey began with a simple curiosity — what does the world look like when you really stop and pay attention?

Over the years I've explored landscapes that breathe, flowers that speak in colour, wildlife moments that pause time, food that tells stories, and the silent drama of human life in motion. My work is rooted in Bangladesh — in its rivers, its rain-washed skies, its resilient people.

The camera is my way of saying: this mattered. This moment was real. This beauty existed.

Specialisations

Photography Interests

The genres I live inside — each demanding a different kind of attention and patience.

🌅

Landscape

From rainbows over Kangabali to misty river sunsets — I chase the precise moment when sky, water, and light conspire to create something unforgettable.

🌸

Flowers & Macro

The world at millimetre scale. A fallen yellow bloom on dark asphalt, a white flower surrounded by golden leaves — beauty that most people walk past without noticing.

🦎

Wildlife

Patience measured in stillness. Learning to disappear into a scene until the wild world forgets I'm there — and then the shutter clicks.

👤

Portrait

A silhouette against a golden sunset. A figure at the edge of the sea. Portraits don't always show faces — sometimes they show souls.

🍽

Food

Food photography is still life with warmth — translating flavour, texture, and the ritual of sharing a meal into a single compelling visual frame.

✨

Miscellaneous

Station platforms, dusty old machines, windows framing hidden worlds — the images that refuse to be categorised and surprise even me.

The Kit

Equipment & Gear

The tools I use to realise a vision — chosen for what they allow me to see, not just what they can do.

📱

Smartphone Camera

Most of this portfolio was shot on a high-end smartphone — proof that the best camera is the one you have with you, and that vision matters more than gear.

🔭

Telephoto / Zoom

For wildlife and compressed landscape shots — reaching into scenes without disturbing them, collapsing distance between subject and sky.

🔍

Macro Mode

Getting close — impossibly close — to flowers, textures, and details that the naked eye skips over. A world within the world.

🎚

Post-Processing

Lightroom and Snapseed for RAW processing, colour grading, and the cinematic final tone that makes each image feel like a frame from a film.

🌟

Natural Light

My most important tool. Golden hour, blue hour, storm light, overcast diffusion — I study light obsessively and build my shoots around it.

🎒

Patience & Presence

The gear that never appears in specs. Arriving early, waiting long, staying still, and being genuinely present in a scene — these make the difference.

The Journey

Photography Timeline

Key moments in my visual journey — from the first shutter click to today.

The Beginning

First Frame

A borrowed phone, an extraordinary sky, and the sudden realisation that I needed to capture what I was seeing. That first photograph changed everything.

Landscapes

Chasing the Sky

Bangladesh has some of the most dramatic skies I've ever seen. Monsoon clouds, rainbows over paddy fields, sunsets over rivers — the landscape became my primary subject.

Macro Discovery

The Small World

I turned the camera downward — to flowers, insects, textures. A fallen bloom on asphalt. Dew on a petal. An entirely new universe opened at my feet.

Wildlife & Portraits

Living Subjects

Learning to photograph living things requires patience above all else. Whether a wild animal or a human silhouette against a sunset — the decisive moment is everything.
